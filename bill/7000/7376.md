**Council Bill Number: 7376**
   
**Ordinance Number: 20151**
********

 An Ordinance repealing Ordinance No. 17321, entitled "An Ordinance providing for the laying off, opening, widening, extending and establishing of Twenty-sixth Avenue South in the City of Seattle, from Dearborn Street to a point eighty-four (84) feet south thereof, as a public street and highway, over and across Lots One (1) and Two (2), in Block One (1) of New Wilfred Addition to the City of Seattle, and providing for the taking and damaging of all land and other property necessary for laying off, opening, widening, extending and establishing of said Twenty-sixth Avenue South in the City of Seattle, across Lots one (1) and two (2) in block one (1) of New Wilfred Addition; and providing for the ascertainment and payment of just compensation to be made for the property and property rights taken or damaged for said purposes and for the assessment upon the property benefited for the purpose of making such compensation and paying the costs of said proceedings; and repealing all ordinances and parts of ordinances in conflict herewith," published October 7, 1907, and directing the dismissal of condemnation proceedings taken by virtue of said ordinance, and appropriating the sum of $12 from the General Fund to pay cost therein.
