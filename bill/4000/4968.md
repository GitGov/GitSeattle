**Council Bill Number: 4968**
   
**Ordinance Number: 18097**
********

 An ordinance repealing Ordinance No. 1144 of the City of Ballard (now part of the City of Seattle) entitled: "An ordinance providing for the opening, altering, widening, extending and establishing of Leary Avenue, in the City of Ballard, as a public street and highway, beginning at the intersection of Leary Avenue and Broadway, and extending along said Leary Avenue to its intersection with 1st Avenue; thence lover and across certain lots, blocks and tracts of land in Gilman Park and in the Northwest quarter of section 13, township 25 north, range 3 east, to the south line produced easterly of lot 11, block 1, Ballard Tide Lands, and providing for the taking and damaging of land and other property necessary therefor, and for the ascertainment and payment of the just compensation to be made for the private property to be taken or damaged for said purpose, and for an assessment upon the property benefited for the purpose of making such compensation" approved September 12, 1906, and authorizing the dismissal of the action in the Superior Court based upon said ordinance and the payment of all taxable costs.
