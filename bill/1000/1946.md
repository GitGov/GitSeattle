**Council Bill Number: 1946**
   
**Ordinance Number: 15249**
********

 An Ordinance providing for the improvement of Bellevue Avenue North, from East Denny Way to Bellevue Place; Summit Avenue North, from East Thomas Street to East Park Drive; Belmont Avenue North, from Lakeview Avenue to East Roy Street; Belmont Place, from East Prospect Street to Belmont Avenue North; Boylston Avenue North, from East Roy Street to East Prospect Street; Harvard Avenue North, from East Roy Street to East Highland Drive; North Broadway, from East Roy Street to East Highland Drive; East Highland Drive, from Harvard Avenue to Tenth Avenue North; East Prospect Street, from Belmont Place to Eleventh Avenue North; East Park Drive, from Summit Avenue North to Boylston Avenue North; Bellevue Place, from Bellevue Avenue North to Boylston Avenue North; East Aloha Street, from Boylston Avenue North to North Broadway; East Roy Street, from Bellevue Avenue North to North Broadway; East Mercer Street, from Bellevue Avenue North to Belmont Avenue North; East Republican Street from Bellevue Avenue North to Belmont Avenue North; East Harrison Street, from the alley west of Bellevue Avenue North to Belmont Avenue North; East Thomas Street, from Bellevue Avenue North to Belmont Avenue North, all in the City of Seattle, by paving the same with asphalt laid upon a proper concrete foundation; by constructing concrete curbs with granite corners and brick gutters; including all grading and regrading; the repairing, alteration and construction of concrete sidewalks where necessary, together with the construction of all water mains and sewers, with appurtenances, necessary for the proper water service fire protection, drainage and sewerage of said district, provided, however, that where the grades exceed ten (10) per cent, pavements shall be of sandstone blocks laid upon proper concrete foundation; all in accordance with Resolution No. 344 of the City Council of the City of Seattle, and providing for the payment therefor by the mode of "Payment by Bonds" as provided by the laws of the State of Washington and the Charter and Ordinances of the City of Seattle.
