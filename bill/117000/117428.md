 AN ORDINANCE relating to the First Hill Streetcar project; authorizing the Director of Transportation to acquire, accept, and record, on behalf of the City of Seattle, a permanent easement for street purposes from the Seattle School District No. 1, a municipal corporation of the State of Washington; and ratifying and confirming certain prior acts.

 WHEREAS, to improve public transit in the City of Seattle, citizens need access to a variety of transit options; and

 WHEREAS, the Sound Transit Board of Directors adopted Sound Transit 2, A Mass Transit Guide, the Regional Transit System Plan for Central Puget Sound on July 24, 2008, which includes capital and operating contributions for streetcar connector lines between downtown Seattle, First Hill, and the future Capitol Hill station; and

 WHEREAS, in December of 2008 the City Council adopted Resolution 31091 confirming the City Council's support for developing a streetcar network in the City of Seattle and identifying the most promising streetcar routes, including the First Hill Streetcar; and

 WHEREAS, the First Hill Streetcar project (the "Project") will provide a modern streetcar line linking First Hill employment centers with the Regional link light rail system by connecting Capitol Hill, First Hill, and Chinatown/International District neighborhoods and provides planning resources for a transit connection between the First Hill Streetcar and the South Lake Union Streetcar; and

 WHEREAS, the First Hill Streetcar route will operate primarily along Broadway, Yesler Way, and South Jackson Street with ten proposed stop locations; and

 WHEREAS, the Project includes construction of two and one half miles of embedded track; roadway improvements to meet the requirements of the Americans with Disabilities Act, the City Complete Streets Ordinance, and the street restoration policy requirements; traction power and overhead contact systems; traffic signal modifications; a storage and light maintenance facility; and public utility modifications; and

 WHEREAS, the City requires a permanent easement for street purposes from the Seattle School District No. 1 for track alignment purposes for the Project; NOW, THEREFORE,

 BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CITY OF SEATTLE AS FOLLOWS:

 Section 1. The Director of Transportation ("Director"), or his designee, is authorized, on behalf of the City of Seattle, to acquire a permanent easement for street purposes, from the Seattle School District No. 1, for fair market value, in substantially the form of the easement attached hereto as Attachment 1, over, under, upon and across the following described real property in Seattle, King County, Washington (a portion of tax parcel No. 000760-0137):

 That portion of the Northeast Quarter of the Northeast Quarter of Section 5, Township 24 North, Range 4 East, W.M., described as follows:

 Beginning at the intersection of the Westerly Right- of-Way margin of 14th Avenue South with the Southerly Right-of-Way margin of East Yesler Way;

 Thence South 1 degrees 15'11" West along said Westerly margin of 14th Avenue South, a distance of 22.51 feet;

 Thence North 41 degrees 28'45" West, a distance of 22.35 feet;

 Thence North 76 degrees 04'23" West, a distance of 27.74 feet to said Southerly margin of East Yesler Way;

 Thence South 88 degrees 44'52" East along said Southerly margin, a distance of 42.23 feet to the Point of Beginning;

 Containing an area of 300 square feet, more or less;

 Situate in the City of Seattle, King County, Washington.

 Section 2. The Director, or his designee, is authorized to execute such documents as he deems necessary or desirable to accomplish acquisition of the easement for street purposes consistent with this ordinance. Upon delivery of the easement for street purposes, the Director or his designee is authorized to accept the easement on behalf of the City by attaching to the easement his written acceptance thereof, and to record the same. The easement property rights shall be accepted for street purposes and placed under the jurisdiction of the Seattle Department of Transportation.

 Section 3. Any acts consistent with the authority and prior to the effective date of this ordinance are hereby ratified and confirmed.

 Section 4. This ordinance shall take effect and be in force 30 days after its approval by the Mayor, but if not approved and returned by the Mayor within ten days after presentation, it shall take effect as provided by Seattle Municipal Code Section 1.04.020.
