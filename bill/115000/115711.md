 ORDINANCE _________________

 AN ORDINANCE relating to the Neighborhood Parks, Green Spaces, Trails and Zoo Levy; authorizing the acquisition of a portion of a condominium unit located within the International District Village Square 2 condominium project for an expansion of the International District/Chinatown Community Center; authorizing acceptance of the property for park and recreation purposes; and increasing appropriations to the Department of Parks and Recreation in the 2006 Budget; all by a three fourths vote of the City Council.

 WHEREAS, pursuant to Ordinance 121251 and using funds provided by the 1999 Seattle Center and Community Center Levy, in 2003 the City of Seattle ("City") acquired Condominium Unit No. 2 in IDVS2, a Condominium, for the International District/Chinatown Community Center ("IDCC"); and

 WHEREAS, in 2000, Ordinance 120024 proposed the Neighborhood Parks, Green Spaces, Trails, and Zoo Levy (hereafter, "2000 Parks Levy") to provide funding for, among other things, property acquisitions and park improvement projects identified by neighborhood and community groups; and

 WHEREAS, the 2000 Parks Levy program includes an Acquisition and Development Opportunity Fund to fund acquisition and development projects not identified for funding in the remainder of the 2000 Parks Levy program; and

 WHEREAS, in Ordinance 121947 the Seattle City Council approved the 2000 Parks Levy Oversight Committee's award of One Hundred Eight Thousand Dollars ($108,000) from the 2000 Parks Levy Fund for the acquisition of additional property at IDVS2 for an expansion of the IDCC and authorized the Superintendent of Parks and Recreation to proceed with such acquisition; and

 WHEREAS, Seattle Chinatown/International District Preservation and Development Authority (SCIDPDA), the owner of IDVS2 Condominium Unit No.3, has agreed to sell 491 square feet of Unit No. 3 (hereafter, the "Additional Space") to the City for One Hundred Thousand Dollars ($100,000); and

 WHEREAS, the Additional Space will be conveyed to the City by the recording of an amended Condominium Declaration and an amended Survey Map and Plan for IDVS2, which documents will cause the Additional Space to be removed from the legal description of Unit No. 3 and added to the legal description of Unit No. 2; NOW, THEREFORE,

 BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CITY OF SEATTLE AS FOLLOWS:

 Section 1. The Superintendent of Parks and Recreation or his designee is authorized to acquire, on behalf of the City, that real property commonly known as the International District Community Center Annex and described in Attachment 1 hereto, (the "Property"), in accordance with the terms and conditions of Attachment 1, the International District Community Center Annex Real Estate Purchase and Sales Agreement.

 Section 2. The Superintendent or his designee is authorized to execute such documents as such official deems necessary or desirable to complete the purchase of the Property consistent with this ordinance. The Property shall be accepted for park and recreation purposes and placed under the jurisdiction of the Seattle Department of Parks and Recreation.

 Section 3. To pay for the acquisition authorized in Section 1 above and for necessary related costs and expenses, for which insufficient appropriations were made, the following appropriation amount is added to the 2006 Budget of the DPR:

 Fund Department Budget Control Amount Level/Capital Improvement Program

 2000 Parks Levy Parks and 2000 Parks Levy - $108,000 Fund (33850) Recreation Opportunity Fund Acquisitions Program K723007

 Section 4. This ordinance shall take effect and be in force thirty (30) days from and after its approval by the Mayor, but if not approved and returned by the Mayor within ten (10) days after presentation, it shall take effect as provided by Municipal Code Section 1.04.020.

 Attachment 1

 IDVS2 Annex Purchase and Sales Agreement

 This Purchase and Sales Agreement ("PSA"), is entered into this day of

 2006, by and among Seattle Chinatown International District Public Development Authority (SCIDPDA), referred to herein as "Seller", and The City of Seattle, referred to herein as "Buyer"

 1. Property. The "Property" consists of an approximately 491 square foot retail bay located along 8th Avenue South in Seattle, Washington on Level One of IDVS2, a Condominium recorded under recording number 20021211002628 and in Volume 187 of Condominiums, Pages 14 through 25 inclusive (the "Condominium"). The Property is adjacent to Unit 2 of the Condominium. No personal property is included in the Property.

 2. Purchase. Seller shall sell and convey the Property to Buyer, and Buyer shall accept the Property from Seller, on the terms, covenants and conditions set forth in this PSA.

 3. Purchase Price. Purchase price shall be $100,000 and said price shall represent the full cost of the portion of the condominium unit described in Section 4 below.

 4. Property Description. Buyer is the owner of Unit 2 of the Condominium. Seller is the Owner of Unit 3 of the Condominium. The Property is currently a portion of Unit 3. Concurrent with the closing of the sale of the Property, Seller will cause to be recorded an amendment to the Declaration of Condominium and Covenants, Conditions and Restrictions for IDVS2, a Condominium, that relocates the boundary of Unit 2 of the Condominium to include the Property (the "Declaration Amendment") in the form attached hereto. The legal description of Unit 2 after such relocation shall be:

 Unit 2, IDVS2, a condominium, according to the Condominium Declaration recorded under Recording Number 20021211002628, and amended under Recording Number and in Volume 187 of

 Condominiums, page(s) 14 through 25, inclusive, in King County, Washington.

 4.1 Title. Seller shall obtain, at Buyer's expense, a preliminary commitment for an ALTA owner's renewal policy of title insurance ("Title Report") on the Property, together with a copy of every document underlying any encumbrance to title shown as a special exception on Schedule B of the Title Report. Except for liens or encumbrances created by the condominium declaration and building or zoning regulations and any printed standard exceptions, all of which shall be deemed to be and referred to herein as "Permitted Exceptions". All other encumbrances including, but not limited to, any mortgage, deed of trust, or other debt security, or any attachment, delinquent real estate tax, assessment for an improvement or mechanic's or materialmen's lien outstanding against the Property shall be exceptions to title that Seller hereby covenants to remove or cause to be satisfied on or before the Closing Date.

 5. Deliveries to Escrow Holder.

 5.1. By Seller. Seller shall deliver or cause to be delivered into escrow number

 ("Escrow") with Pacific Northwest Title Insurance Company ("Escrow

 Holder" or "Title Company", as the case may be) on or before the Closing Date the following items:

 (a) Declaration Amendment. Seller shall convey the Property to Buyer by recording the Declaration Amendment. A condition precedent to Buyer's completing the transaction shall be the willingness of Title Company to issue, upon payment of Title Company's regularly scheduled premium, an Owner's title insurance policy ("Real Property Title Policy") in a form acceptable to Buyer and in the amount of $100,000. Subject only to the Permitted Exceptions and any other matters that Buyer has approved in writing. The Real Property Title Policy shall contain such special endorsements and affirmative assurances as Buyer may reasonably require.

 6. Close of Escrow.

 6.1. Time. Provided that all conditions precedent to Closing have been satisfied or waived, the close of escrow (the "Closing") shall occur on a date mutually agreed upon by the parties that is not more than fifteen (15) days following the effective date of an Ordinance authorizing Buyer to acquire the property interests described herein (the "Closing Date"). When all documents and funds required hereunder have been deposited with Escrow Holder, Escrow Holder shall immediately close Escrow as provided for below.

 6.2. Procedure. Escrow Holder shall close escrow as follows:

 (a) Obtain the release of the Property from any liens except the Permitted Exceptions;

 (b) Record the Declaration Amendment;

 (c) Issue and deliver the title policy, if any, requested by Buyer;

 (d) Deliver any other documents deposited by Seller into escrow to Buyer; and forward to Buyer and Seller, in duplicate, a separate accounting of all funds received and disbursed for each party and copies of all executed and recorded or filed documents deposited into escrow, with such recording and filing date endorsed thereon.

 (e) Seller shall deposit a FIRPTA Affidavit executed by Seller, evidencing that Seller is exempt from the withholding requirements of Section 1445 of the Internal Revenue Code.

 (f) Seller shall deposit such certificates as are necessary or required by the Escrow Holder to evidence the authority of Seller and its signatories to execute the instruments to be executed by Seller in connection with this transaction.

 6.3. Incorporation of Escrow Instructions. This PSA shall serve as escrow instructions, and an executed copy of this PSA shall be deposited by Buyer with Escrow Holder following its execution. The parties may execute additional escrow instructions provided that such additional escrow instructions shall not change the terms of this PSA.

 6.4. Closing Costs.

 Closing Costs. Buyer and Seller shall each pay their own attorneys' fees. Buyer shall pay the cost of recording the Declaration Amendment, Real Property Title Policy and the escrow fees. There should be no real estate excise or use taxes payable with respect to the transfer.

 6.5 Possession. Seller shall deliver possession of the Property to Buyer upon Closing.

 7. Brokers., Neither party had a broker working for it in connection with the transfer of the Property. Each of parties hereto shall be responsible for all other commissions and finders' fees to which they agreed in writing and shall defend and indemnify the other party hereto against the claims therefor.

 8. As Is. EXCEPT AS STATED IN THIS PSA, BUYER ACKNOWLEDGES THAT IT WILL BE ACCEPTING THE PROPERTY IN RELIANCE ON ITS OWN INSPECTIONS AND "AS IS" AND "WHERE IS". SELLER MAKES NO REPRESENTATIONS OR WARRANTIES WITH RESPECT TO THE PROPERTY OR ITS SUITABILITY FOR BUYER'S INTENDED USE.

 9. Default; Remedies. Specific Performance. This agreement pertains to the conveyance of real property, the unique nature of which is hereby acknowledged by the parties. Consequently, in the event of a breach or default in or of any provision of this agreement by either party, the non-defaulting party shall have, in addition to all other available remedies, the right to specific performance of this PSA.

 14. Counterparts. This PSA may be executed in counterparts with each counterpart so executed deemed an original hereof.

 IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the parties have caused this Agreement to be executed as of the day and year first above written.
