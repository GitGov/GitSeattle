 AN ORDINANCE relating to historic preservation, imposing controls upon Fire Station #13, a landmark designated by the Landmarks Preservation Board under Chapter 25.12 of the Seattle Municipal Code, and adding it to the Table of Historical Landmarks contained in Chapter 25.32 of the Seattle Municipal Code.

 WHEREAS, the Landmarks Ordinance, Chapter 25.12 of the Seattle Municipal Code (SMC), establishes a procedure for the designation and preservation of sites, improvements and objects having historical, cultural, architectural, engineering or geographic significance; and

 WHEREAS, the Landmarks Preservation Board (the Board), after a public meeting on December 1, 2004, voted to approve the nomination of the improvement (the building) and a portion of the parcel of property on which the improvement is located at 3601 Beacon Avenue South in Seattle for designation as a landmark under SMC Chapter 25.12; and

 WHEREAS, after a public meeting on January 5, 2005, the Board voted to approve the designation of Fire Station #13 under SMC Chapter 25.12; and

 WHEREAS, on September 21, 2005, the Board and the owner of the designated landmark agreed to controls and incentives; and

 WHEREAS, on February 17, 2010, the Board approved the legal description for the portion of the legal parcel that describes the designated site; and

 WHEREAS, the Board recommends that the City Council enact a designating ordinance approving the controls and incentives;

 NOW, THEREFORE,

 BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CITY OF SEATTLE AS FOLLOWS:

 Section 1. DESIGNATION: Pursuant to SMC 25.12.660, the designation by the Landmarks Preservation Board (the Board) of the improvement (the building) and a portion of the parcel of property on which the improvement is located, collectively referred to as Fire Station #13 for the purposes of this ordinance, and physically located at 3601 Beacon Avenue South in Seattle, is hereby acknowledged.

 A. Legal Description.

 1. Fire Station #13 is located on a portion of the parcel legally described as:

 Section 16, Township 24 North, Range 4, East W.M, described as follows: That portion of SW 1/4 of NW 1/4, lying west of Beacon Avenue South and that portion of NW 1/4 of SW 1/4 lying west of Beacon Avenue South and further described as follows: from the south line of South Spokane Street as it now exist at the intersection of the south line of South Spokane Street and the East line of 16th Avenue South as vacated under Ordinance 86853 which is the west section line of the southwest quarter section and the point of beginning, then south along the west section line parallel to 16th Avenue South as vacated under Ordinance 86853 to a point on the quarter corner, then 429.50' south along the west section line parallel to 16th Avenue South to a point on the west section line, then east, parallel to South Spokane Street as originally laid out to the west margin of Beacon Avenue South, then northwest along the west margin of Beacon Avenue South to a point where the west margin of Beacon Avenue South intersects with the south margin of South Spokane Street, then west along the south margin of South Spokane Street to the point of beginning.

 2. The portion of the parcel to be designated as a Landmark is legally described as follows:

 That portion of the southwest quarter of the northwest quarter of Section 16, Township 24 North, Range 4 East, W.M. lying west of Beacon Avenue South and south of South Spokane Street in King County Washington; described as follows:

 Commencing at the center line intersection of South Spokane Street and Beacon Avenue South; thence North 88 degrees 52'18" West along the center line of South Spokane Street a distance of 64.33 feet to an intersection with the west margin of Beacon Avenue South prolonged from the south; thence South 10 degrees 33'16" East, along said prolonged west margin a distance of 33.70 feet to the True Point of Beginning;

 Thence continuing South 10 degrees 33'16" East along said west margin a distance of 84.73 feet to an intersection with a line that is parallel with and 35 feet southerly from the existing south wall of Fire Station no. 13;

 Thence South 79 degrees 30'22" West along said parallel line a distance of 90.85 feet;

 Thence North 43 degrees 43'50" West, a distance of 47.25feet;

 Thence North 88 degrees 44'16" West, a distance of 64.00 feet;

 Thence North 01 degrees 40'07" East, a distance of 67.63 feet to the south margin of said South Spokane Street.

 Thence South 88 degrees 52'18" East, along said south margin a distance of 168.52 feet to the True Point of Beginning.

 B. Specific Features and/or Characteristics Designated. Pursuant to SMC 25.12.660.A.2, the following specific features and/or characteristics of Fire Station #13 are designated:

 1. The exterior of the building, excluding the 1986-87 addition, and

 2. The parcel of property legally described above in Section 1.A, located at 3601 Beacon Avenue South in Seattle.

 C. Basis of Designation. The designation was made because Fire Station #13 has significant character, interest or value as a part of the development, heritage or cultural characteristics of the City, state or nation, it has integrity or the ability to convey its significance, and because it satisfies the following from SMC 25.12.350:

 1. It is associated in a significant way with a significant aspect of the cultural, political, or economic heritage of the community, City, state or nation (SMC 25.12.350.C).

 2. It embodies the distinctive visible characteristics of an architectural style, period, or of a method of construction (SMC 25.12.350.D).

 3. Because of its prominence of spatial location, contrasts of siting, age, or scale, it is an easily identifiable visual feature of its neighborhood or the City and contributes to the distinctive quality or identity of such neighborhood or the City (SMC 25.12.350.F).

 Section 2. CONTROLS: The following controls are hereby imposed on the features and characteristics of Fire Station #13 that were designated by the Board for preservation:

 A. Certificate of Approval Process.

 1. Except as provided in Section 2.A.2 of this ordinance, the owner must obtain a Certificate of Approval issued by the Board pursuant to SMC Chapter 25.12, or the time for denying a Certificate of Approval must have expired, before the owner may make alterations or significant changes to the following specific features or characteristics:

 a. The exterior of the improvement (the building), excluding the 1986-87 addition, and

 b. The portion of the parcel of property legally described in Section 1.A.2, located at 3601 Beacon Avenue South in Seattle.

 2. No Certificate of Approval is required for the following:

 a. Any in-kind maintenance or repairs of the features listed in Section 2.A.1.

 b. Minor landscaping, including the removal or addition of trees under six (6) inches in caliper, shrubs, perennials and annuals.

 B. City Historic Preservation Officer (CHPO) Approval Process.

 1. The CHPO may review and approve the items listed in Section 2.B.3 of this Ordinance according to the following procedure:

 a. The owner shall submit to the City Historic Preservation Officer (CHPO) a written request for the alterations, including applicable drawings and/or specifications.

 b. If the CHPO, upon examination of submitted plans and specifications, determines that the alterations are consistent with the purposes of SMC Chapter 25.12, the alterations shall be approved without further action by the Board.

 c. If the CHPO does not approve the alterations, the owner may submit revised materials to the CHPO, or apply to the Board for a Certificate of Approval under SMC Chapter 25.12, as provided in Section 2.A.

 2. The CHPO shall transmit a written decision on the owner's request to the owner within 14 days of receipt of the request. Failure of the CHPO to approve or disapprove the request shall constitute approval of the request.

 3. CHPO approval for changes or alterations to the designated features or characteristics of the landmark described in Section 1.B of this Ordinance, is available for the following:

 a. The addition or elimination of duct conduits, HVAC vents, grilles, fire escapes, pipes, and other similar wiring or mechanical elements necessary for the normal operation of the building.

 b. The addition or elimination of Seattle Fire Department training equipment attached to the designated features.

 Section 3. INCENTIVES: The following incentives are hereby granted on the features and characteristics of Fire Station #13 that were designated by the Board for preservation:

 A. Uses not otherwise permitted in a zone may be authorized in a designated Landmark by means of an administrative conditional use issued pursuant to Seattle Municipal Code Title 23.

 B. Exceptions to certain requirements of the Seattle Building Code, SMC Chapter 22.100, and the Energy Code of the City, SMC Chapter 22.700, may be authorized pursuant to the applicable provisions thereof.

 C. Special tax valuation for historic preservation may be available under Chapter 84.26 RCW upon application and compliance with the requirements of that statute.

 Section 4. Enforcement of this ordinance and penalties for its violation are as provided in SMC 25.12.910.

 Section 5. Fire Station #13 is hereby added to the Table of Historical Landmarks contained in SMC Chapter 25.32.

 Section 6. The City Clerk is directed to record a certified copy of the ordinance with the King County Director of Records and Elections, deliver two certified copies to the City Historic Preservation Officer (CHPO), and deliver one copy to the Director of the Department of Planning and Development. The CHPO is directed to provide a certified copy of the ordinance to the owner of the landmark.

 Section 7. This ordinance shall take effect and be in force thirty (30) days from and after its approval by the Mayor, but if not approved and returned by the Mayor within ten (10) days after presentation, it shall take effect as provided by Municipal Code Section 1.04.020.
