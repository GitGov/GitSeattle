 AN ORDINANCE relating to the Executive Services Department, Technology Division; accepting an award for the City's Public Access Network (PAN); increasing the expenditure allowance in that department's 1998 budget to support public access to technology and providing payment therefor; all by three-fourths vote of the City Council.

 WHEREAS, the City's Public Access Network (PAN) has won numerous contests and designations as one of the best world wide web sites on the Internet; and

 WHEREAS, Government-On-Line is an information service which provides government executives with summary information about successful programs; and

 WHEREAS, Government-On-Line and several other public and private organizations sponsored a contest to identify the best state and local government web sites; and

 WHEREAS, the City of Seattle's Public Access Network was selected for second place among local governments in this contest, and received a $10,000 award for this selection; and

 WHEREAS, one of PAN's important goals is to provide public sites where citizens can access the Internet, including City government information; and

 WHEREAS, PAN recommends that the award funds be used to support public access projects; NOW, THEREFORE,

 BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CITY OF SEATTLE AS FOLLOWS:

 Section 1. The Executive Services Director is authorized to accept an award of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000) from State Technologies, Inc. as a result of the Public Access Network's placing second in the 1997 Government-On-Line Best of the Web contest. The funds shall be deposited in the Executive Services Fund.

 Section 2. Upon receipt of the funds accepted in Section 1, the expenditure allowance in the 1998 budget of the Executive Services Department, Technology Division (SFMS Code A44), will be increased by the amount of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000) for the purpose of supporting public access to the Internet, by appropriating the sum of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000) from the Executive Services Fund.

 Section 3. Any act consistent with the authority and prior to the effective date of this ordinance is hereby ratified and confirmed.

 Section 4. The foregoing appropriation is made to meet actual necessary expenditures of the City for which insufficient appropriation has been made due to causes which could not reasonably

 have been foreseen at the time of the adoption of the 1998 budget;

 Now, Therefore, in accordance with RCW 35.32A.060, by reason of the facts above stated, this ordinance shall take effect and be in force thirty (30) days from and after its approval by the Mayor, but if not approved and returned by the Mayor within ten (10) days after presentation, it shall take effect as provided by Municipal Code Section 1.04.020.
