 ORDINANCE ______

 AN ORDINANCE related to the acquisition, for open space, park and recreation purposes, of a portion of Lot 4, Block 5, of what is commonly known as Bell and Denny's Addition to the City of Seattle, according to the plat thereof recorded in Volume 1 of Plats, page 29, in King County, Washington; authorizing acquisition and acceptance of the deed; authorizing acquisition by condemnation, if necessary; and reducing and reallocating an appropriation made in Ordinance 118311.

 WHEREAS, The City of Seattle issued $35,000,000.00 in Limited Tax General Obligation Bonds (Capital Facilities Projects), 1992, as authorized by Ordinance 116105, secured by a pledge of Real Estate Excise Tax revenues (the "1992 REET Bonds"); and

 WHEREAS, Ordinance 117100 amended the 1994 Adopted Budget and, in Subsection 1.C., allocated and appropriated up to $650,000 of the proceeds of the 1992 REET Bonds to the Department of Housing and Human Services for p-patches; and

 WHEREAS, Ordinance 118162 authorized the issuance of up to $26,000,000 in Limited Tax General Obligation Bonds (the "1996 Bonds") to pay a portion of the costs of the downtown concert hall, to provide funds for low-income housing, and for other lawful City purposes as determined by ordinance; and

 WHEREAS, Ordinance 118311 in Section 2 decreased the $650,000 appropriation for p-patches made in Ordinance 117100 to zero; and

 WHEREAS, Ordinance 118311 in Section 5 provided that p-patches were eligible for the use of proceeds of the 1996 Bonds, appropriated $650,000 of the proceeds of the 1996 Bonds for p-patches, and provided that funds appropriated for p-patches were to be expended at the direction of the DHHS Director, or such other Department Director to whom responsibility for the P-Patch Program may be transferred; and

 WHEREAS, Ordinance 118546 transferred the expenditure authority for those funds to the Department of Neighborhoods for p-patches, and

 WHEREAS, a portion of those proceeds of the 1996 Bonds remains appropriated but unspent in the budget of the Department of Neighborhoods; and

 WHEREAS, the City has an opportunity to acquire a parcel of property that will join noncontiguous parcels under the jurisdiction of the Department of Parks and Recreation and commonly known as the Belltown P-Patch; and

 WHEREAS, the acquisition is a permissible capital purpose for proceeds of the 1996 Bonds under Ordinance 118311 and the Department of Neighborhoods and the Friends of P-Patch desire to have the necessary portion of the unspent funds reallocated to the Department of Parks and Recreation to complete acquisition of land for the Belltown P- Patch; NOW, THEREFORE,

 BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CITY OF SEATTLE AS FOLLOWS:

 Section 1. The Superintendent of Parks and Recreation is hereby authorized to acquire, for and on behalf of The City of Seattle, in accordance with the terms and provisions of the Real Estate Purchase and Sale Agreement attached hereto and incorporated herein by reference, the following real property (known as OSP No. 304.049, RPAMIS subject parcel number 9735 and a portion of King County Assessor's Tax Parcel No. 063500 0165) situated in the City of Seattle, King County, Washington, to wit:

 The northeasterly 23 feet of the southwesterly 99.51 feet of Lot 4, Block 5, portion of the Town of Seattle as laid out on the Land Claim of William H. Bell and the northwestern extremity of the Claim of A.A. Denny, commonly known as Bell and Denny's Addition to the City of Seattle, according to the plat thereof recorded in Volume 1 of Plats, page 29, in King County, Washington.

 Section 2. Upon delivery of a statutory warranty deed for the real property described in Section 1, the Superintendent of Parks and Recreation, or his designee, is authorized to accept and record the deed on behalf of The City of Seattle for open space, park and recreation purposes.

 Section 3. The purchase price of One Hundred Seventy-nine Thousand Four Hundred Dollars ($179,400), together with any and all costs associated with the acquisition, including but not limited to appraisals, environmental site assessments and closing costs, shall be paid from the funds reappropriated in Section 4 below.

 Section 4. To pay for the necessary costs and expenses identified in Section 3, the sum of One Hundred Eighty-five Thousand Dollars ($185,000) of the proceeds of the City's Limited Tax General Obligation Bonds, 1996, Series A (Tax-Exempt), is hereby reappropriated by reducing the $650,000 appropriation originally made in Section 5 of Ordinance 118311, and subsequently transferred to the Department of Neighborhoods, by One Hundred Eighty-five Thousand Dollars ($185,000) and reallocating such amount to the 1998 budget of the Department of Parks and Recreation, to carry forward as needed until the acquisition authorized in this ordinance is completed or abandoned. Any portion thereof that is unspent upon completion or abandonment of the acquisition described in Section 1 is hereby reallocated to the Department of Neighborhoods to spend for p- patches.

 Section 5. If the City fails to acquire the real property and property rights described in Section 1 above by negotiation, as determined by the Superintendent of Parks and Recreation, the City Attorney is authorized to begin and prosecute proceedings in the manner provided by law to condemn, take, damage and appropriate in fee simple the real property and property rights described in Section 1 for open space, park and recreation purposes. Such real property and other property rights shall be so appropriated and taken only after just compensation has been made or paid into court for the owners thereof in the manner provided by law. The City Attorney is further authorized to stipulate for the purpose of minimizing damages.

 Section 6. The entire cost of the acquisition provided for in Section 5 above shall be paid from general funds of The City of Seattle.

 Section 7. Any act consistent with the authority and prior to the effective date of this ordinance is hereby ratified and confirmed.

 Section 8. This ordinance shall take effect and be in force thirty (30) days from and after its approval by the Mayor, but if not approved and returned by the Mayor within ten (10) days after presentation, it shall take effect as provided by Municipal Code Section 1.04.020.
