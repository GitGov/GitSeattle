 AN ORDINANCE relating to the 2003 Fire Facilities and Emergency Response Levy Program, increasing appropriations to the Fleets and Facilities Department in the 2004 Budget; making a cash transfer from the Cumulative Reserve Subfund to the 2003 Fire Facilities Fund; creating and reclassifying positions; and authorizing the Fleets and Facilities Department to negotiate for the acquisition of real property; all by a three-fourths vote of the City Council.

 WHEREAS, Ordinance 121230 authorized the submission to the voters of Seattle of a proposition approving additional regular property taxes in excess of the limitation on levies in Chapter 84.55 RCW for the purpose of paying all or a part of the cost of neighborhood stations, support facilities, marine apparatus, emergency preparedness improvements, and other emergency response facilities, known collectively as the Fire Facilities and Emergency Response Levy (Levy Program); established estimated funding levels for the 42 named projects that comprise the Levy Program; and authorized the creation of the 2003 Fire Facilities Fund in the City Treasury; and

 WHEREAS, the Levy Program proposed under Ordinance 121230 was approved by Seattle voters on November 4, 2003; and

 WHEREAS, the Mayor's Proposed 2004-2009 Capital Improvement Program (CIP) for the Fleets and Facilities Department identified projects to be carried out in each of those years in the event of Seattle voters' approval of the Levy Program; and

 WHEREAS, the Mayor's Proposed 2004 Budget for the Fleets and Facilities Department did not propose, and the City Council and Mayor did not make, appropriations from the 2003 Fire Facilities Fund, in expectation that these appropriations would be made through separate legislative action following Seattle voters' approval of the Levy Program; and

 WHEREAS, the Port of Seattle provided the City of Seattle a grant of $500,000 for a Fire Department Marine Response Vessel, and such funds plus accumulated interest earnings thereon were appropriated through Ordinance 121002 from the Cumulative Reserve Subfund, Unrestricted Account for such purposes; and

 WHEREAS, the Executive has prepared a Levy Program Implementation Plan, which, among other things, identifies new staff positions and reclassifications desirable and administratively practical for carrying out the Levy Program; and

 WHEREAS, the voter-approved Levy Program calls for the City's timely acquisition of multiple properties necessary for siting certain projects included within the Levy Program; NOW, THEREFORE,

 BE IT ORDAINED BY THE CITY OF SEATTLE AS FOLLOWS:

 Section 7. The Director of the Fleets and Facilities Department is hereby authorized, for and on behalf of The City of Seattle, to negotiate for the acquisition of real property for general municipal purposes for the siting of the fire and emergency response facilities contemplated under the Neighborhood Stations Category and Emergency Preparedness Category of the Fire Facilities and Emergency Response Levy Program. Any purchase and sale agreements for such properties shall be expressly conditioned on City authorization of each acquisition by specific ordinance.

 Section 8. To support the City's successful implementation of the Levy Program and to provide the Fire Facilities Oversight Committee with valuable information on various implementation issues, the Executive shall submit, after consultation with the City Auditor, a proposal for the selection of (a) consultant(s) to provide independent audit analysis of the construction projects associated with the Fire Facilities Levy to the Fire Facilities Oversight Committee. The Executive shall submit the proposal to the Fire Facilities Oversight Committee no later than February 27th of 2004, or if the Committee has not formed by that time, to the President of the City Council. The proposal shall identify a process for selecting the consultant(s), an appropriate funding mechanism, any necessary implementing legislation, and include a detailed work plan for 2004 that may include, but is not limited to, a review of: contract document development; scope management; risk management; contract administration; cost management; schedule management; project management; quality management; project communication/reporting; and document management. In anticipation of the creation of the independent audit resource, the Fleets and Facilities Department is directed to document and maintain records relating to the above potential Levy Program implementation work plan items in addition to other record-keeping requirements and/or practices of the City.

 Section 9. The Director of Fleets and Facilities will provide reports on both a monthly and quarterly basis to the Fire Facility Oversight Committee and any other interested Councilmembers. The information that shall be included in the reports, along with the frequency, is listed in Attachment A.

 Section 10. This ordinance shall take effect and be in force thirty (30) days from and after its approval by the Mayor, but if not approved and returned by the Mayor within ten (10) days after presentation, it shall take effect as provided by Municipal Code Section 1.04.020.

 Project Narrative (Monthly): A narrative description of how individual projects are proceeding and what has been accomplished to date as well as future activities that FFD anticipates will occur in the intervening month.

 Project Schedule (Monthly): Schedules for each project underway that show the original proposed timeline for each phase of work (e.g., preliminary design, final design, scope approval, construction, etc.) and the actual time it has taken or is estimated to take to complete each task. The schedule should clearly show where a project is in the process and whether it is on target to meet the completion date.

 Project Budget & Contingencies (Monthly): The original base budget, original contract, approved changes, adjusted contract, project life to date costs, and remaining budget. Individual and overall project contingencies should be tracked and reported on separately and if any contingency is spent, the report should explain when and why.

 Project Scope and Change Orders (Monthly): A description of preliminary and final project scope when it is developed for each project and if and when there are any Change Order Proposals associated with the scope and what the impact to cost and schedule are. FFD should have independent cost estimates completed for Change Order Proposals.

 Land Acquisition (Quarterly): Progress on FFD's land acquisition plan, including timeline, expenditures to date, remaining budget authority, and the interim management plans for each parcel as they are acquired.

 Grant Funds (Quarterly): FFD should report on any additional grant funds it receives that were not previously assumed in the Levy and report on what they can be used for and if and how much levy funds are "freed" up as a result.

 Overall Levy Progress (Quarterly): A schedule analysis that gives a snapshot of how the overall levy program is progressing to enable Council to see how the different pieces and timetables relate to each other.

 BOOST and Apprenticeship Usage (Quarterly): For contracted work, the BOOST and apprenticeship usage for each program area (i.e., Neighborhood stations, "Command Center", Marine, etc).
